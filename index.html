About Me

From technology operations to educational research—my journey in bridging digital innovation and human learning

Technology has always intrigued me—not as an abstract concept, but as a force that shapes how we connect, learn, and navigate the world. In China, where I grew up, the digital revolution wasn't something I read about; it was something I lived through. Platforms like Douyin and Xiaohongshu didn't just emerge—they wove themselves into the fabric of everyday life, transforming how people share stories, discover ideas, and build knowledge together.

My path toward understanding technology and learning didn't start in an academic setting. Instead, it began in the fast-paced world of digital media. At Hangzhou Rolling Elephant Network Technology Co. Ltd., I managed the operations of three major platforms: Douyin (TikTok's Chinese counterpart), TikTok, and Rednote (Xiaohongshu). Working at the intersection of technology and human behavior, I saw how digital spaces could spark engagement and facilitate learning in unexpected ways. Yet this also made me ask harder questions: How do we design these experiences thoughtfully? What makes a digital learning environment truly effective?

These questions brought me to the Master's programme in IT and Learning at the University of Gothenburg. Here, I'm building the theoretical understanding and practical capabilities needed to become a professional in technology-enhanced learning. My goal is to approach digital tools with a critical eye—examining not just what they can do, but how they should be designed to create meaningful learning experiences, especially within organisational settings.

Research | Literature Review | Early Childhood Education | Educational Technology

The Impact of Animated Videos on Preschool Children's Language Development

Project Overview

This course paper examines how animated videos—increasingly prevalent on platforms like YouTube, TikTok, and streaming services—affect preschool children's language development. Through a systematic literature review following PRISMA guidelines, I analyzed five empirical studies to answer: Do animated videos help or hinder vocabulary acquisition? What factors moderate these effects? How do these findings inform educational technology design?

Literature Review PRISMA Methodology Early Childhood Language Development Educational Technology Research Methods
Context & Motivation

This research topic emerged from the convergence of my professional experience and personal concerns. At Hangzhou Rolling Elephant Network Technology, I managed content operations for TikTok and Douyin, witnessing millions of young children consuming short-form video content. As a mother, I faced daily decisions about my child's media use. I wanted evidence-based answers grounded in learning theories like Vygotsky's sociocultural theory, Piaget's cognitive development stages, Mayer's multimedia learning principles, and Sweller's cognitive load theory.

Research Process
  • Conducted systematic literature review following PRISMA guidelines
  • Searched Scopus and ERIC databases with comprehensive search strategy
  • Six-stage screening process: 196 articles → 5 final studies
  • Applied rigorous inclusion criteria focusing on empirical studies from 2024-2025
  • Analyzed findings through multiple theoretical lenses
Key Findings

The research revealed that animated videos can support preschool children's language development when designed thoughtfully. Critical factors include adult co-viewing and mediation, age-appropriate content design, interactive elements that promote engagement, cultural and linguistic considerations, and balancing entertainment with educational value. The findings emphasize that technology's educational impact depends on design quality and social context.

Theoretical Frameworks Applied
  • Vygotsky's Sociocultural Theory (role of adult mediation)
  • Piaget's Cognitive Development Stages (age-appropriate design)
  • Mayer's Multimedia Learning Principles (effective content design)
  • Sweller's Cognitive Load Theory (managing complexity)
